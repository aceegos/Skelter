SKELTER RULES

Each player is dealt seven cards. The aim is to shed every card in your hand before your opponent.

Players take one turn at a time and may play one legal card or draw one card from the draw pile. Drawing a card completes the turn. The drawn card cannot be played immediately.

A card may be played by following the suit of the current table card, matching its value, or playing a numbered card that adds with the table card to make 10.

The first player to take a turn in a game cannot begin with an Ace, 10 or Joker. After that first turn, either player may play an Ace, 10 or Joker on any of their turns.

Ace, 10 and Joker get you the House. If you already have the House, you keep the House. The House allows you to play court cards that follow the current suit.

If a court card is on the table and the other player draws, the player with the House may next play another court card of the same value or a court card of the same suit.

A Joker is wild. The player who plays it chooses the active suit. The Joker also gets the House.

When you finish your turn with one card left, you must call SKELTER! before your next turn. If you fail to call SKELTER, you cannot win with that final card and must draw a card.

The first player to shed their final card wins immediately.
